Braille Symbol: Minus (-)

Composition of Braille 'Minus'

Braille 'Minus' symbol is formed by **dots 3, 6 (bottom left, bottom right) and dots 3, 6 (bottom left, bottom right)** in the 6-dot Braille system.

It is represented across two cells, with both cells consisting of dots 3, 6.

Reading Order

For the Braille 'Minus' symbol, check the bottom left and bottom right dots in both the first and second cells.

Check the presence of dots from left to right, and from top to bottom.

Other Explanations

The minus symbol is used in mathematics to denote the subtraction operation.

It is used to express the difference between numbers or to represent negative numbers.
