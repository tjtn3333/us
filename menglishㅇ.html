Braille Consonant: O/NG

Composition of Braille 'O/NG'

Braille 'O/NG' is formed by **dots 1, 2, 5, 6** of the 6 dots.

Reading Order

To read Braille 'O/NG', identify the top left, middle left, middle right, and bottom right dots.

Other Explanations

The Korean consonant 'ㅇ' (O/NG) has no sound when used as an initial consonant, but makes an 'ng' sound when used as a final consonant. It is an important consonant for learning braille. This braille character is formed by **dots 1, 2, 5, 6** based on the 6-dot braille system.
