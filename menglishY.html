Braille Alphabet: Y

Composition of Braille 'Y'

Braille 'Y' is formed by **dots 1, 3, 4, 5, 6** of the 6 dots.

It uses the same dot pattern as the Korean vowel 'ã…¡', so it must be used with a character table to be recognized as an alphabet.

Reading Order

Braille 'Y' is formed by **dots 1, 3, 4, 5, 6** in all left dots and all right dots of the 6 dots.

Start from the upper left and check the dots.

Other Explanations

Alphabet 'Y' is the twenty-fifth letter of the English alphabet and one of the basic alphabets in Braille learning.

This Braille character is formed by **dots 1, 3, 4, 5, 6** based on the 6-dot Braille system.
