Braille Alphabet: V

Composition of Braille 'V'

Braille 'V' is formed by **dots 1, 2, 3, 6** of the 6 dots.

It uses the same dot pattern as the Korean vowel 'ã… ', so it must be used with a character table to be recognized as an alphabet.

Reading Order

Braille 'V' is formed by **dots 1, 2, 3, 6** in all left dots and lower right of the 6 dots.

Start from the upper left and check the dots.

Other Explanations

Alphabet 'V' is the twenty-second letter of the English alphabet and one of the basic alphabets in Braille learning.

This Braille character is formed by **dots 1, 2, 3, 6** based on the 6-dot Braille system.
