Braille Symbol: Equal (=)

Composition of Braille 'Equal'

Braille 'Equal' symbol is formed by **dots 2, 3, 5, 6 (middle left, bottom left, middle right, bottom right)** in the 6-dot Braille system.

This symbol is represented across two cells.

Reading Order

For the Braille 'Equal' symbol, check the middle left, bottom left, middle right, and bottom right dots in both the first and second cells.

Check the presence of dots from top to bottom, and from left to right.

Other Explanations

The equal symbol is used in mathematics to denote equality.

It is used to indicate that two values or expressions are the same.
