Braille Number: 9

Composition of Braille 9

To represent the number '9' in braille, first, include the
**number sign (dots 3-4-5-6)**, then
follow it with **braille 'I' (dots 2-4)**.

Since it uses the same dot pattern as braille 'I',
it must be used with the number sign to be recognized as a number.

Reading Order

Braille '9' is formed by **dots 2 and 4**
of the 6-dot cell.

Start from the top left
and check the dots downwards.

Other Explanations

The number '9' represents nine units.

It is a common braille number used in various counting and numerical tasks.

This braille character, based on a 6-dot cell,
has **dots 2 and 4** raised.
