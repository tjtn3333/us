Braille Alphabet: X

Composition of Braille 'X'

Braille 'X' is formed by **dots 1, 3, 4, 6** of the 6 dots.

It uses the same dot pattern as the Korean vowel 'ã…Ÿ', so it must be used with a character table to be recognized as an alphabet.

Reading Order

Braille 'X' is formed by **dots 1, 3, 4, 6** in the upper left, lower left, upper right, and lower right of the 6 dots.

Start from the upper left and check the dots.

Other Explanations

Alphabet 'X' is the twenty-fourth letter of the English alphabet and one of the basic alphabets in Braille learning.

This Braille character is formed by **dots 1, 3, 4, 6** based on the 6-dot Braille system.
