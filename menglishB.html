Braille Alphabet: B

Composition of Braille 'B'

Braille 'B' is formed by **dots 1, 2** of the 6 dots.

It uses the same dot pattern as the Korean consonant 'ã„´', so it must be used with a character table to be recognized as an alphabet.

Reading Order

Braille 'B' is formed by **dots 1, 2** in the upper left and middle left of the 6 dots.

Start from the upper left and move down to check the dots.

Other Explanations

Alphabet 'B' is the second letter of the English alphabet and one of the basic alphabets in Braille learning.

This Braille character is formed by **dots 1, 2** based on the 6-dot Braille system.
