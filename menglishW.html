Braille Alphabet: W

Composition of Braille 'W'

Braille 'W' is formed by **dots 2, 4, 5, 6** of the 6 dots.

It uses the same dot pattern as the Korean vowel '„Öù', so it must be used with a character table to be recognized as an alphabet.

Reading Order

Braille 'W' is formed by **dots 2, 4, 5, 6** in the middle left, upper right, middle right, and lower right of the 6 dots.

Start from the middle left and check the dots.

Other Explanations

Alphabet 'W' is the twenty-third letter of the English alphabet and one of the basic alphabets in Braille learning.

This Braille character is formed by **dots 2, 4, 5, 6** based on the 6-dot Braille system.
