Braille Symbol: Plus (+)

Composition of Braille 'Plus'

Braille 'Plus' symbol is formed by **dots 3, 4, 6 (bottom left, top right, bottom right) and dots 1, 3, 6 (top left, bottom left, bottom right)** in the 6-dot Braille system.

It is represented across two cells, with the first cell having dots 3, 4, 6 and the second cell having dots 1, 3, 6.

Reading Order

For the Braille 'Plus' symbol, check the bottom left, top right, and bottom right dots in the first cell, and then the top left, bottom left, and bottom right dots in the second cell.

Check the presence of dots from left to right, and from top to bottom.

Other Explanations

The plus symbol is used in mathematics to denote the addition operation.

It is used to express an additive relationship between numbers or in mathematical expressions.
